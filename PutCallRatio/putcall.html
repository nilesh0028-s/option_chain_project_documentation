What is Put Call Ratio?

Put–Call Ratio (PCR) is a market‑sentiment indicator that compares how many put options are being traded (or are open) to how many call options are being traded (or are open) for the same index or stock. It tells you whether traders, as a group, are more focused on protection/downside (puts) or on upside (calls).

How to Read the PCR Chart

X-Axis (Horizontal Axis)

The X-axis shows time.

  • Each point represents a time interval (here: 15 minutes)
  • It shows how PCR and NIFTY price change during the trading day

Example:

  • 9:30 → Market open
  • 16:00 → Market close

Left Y-Axis (PCR Axis)

The left Y-axis represents the Put-Call Ratio (PCR).

  • Values usually range between 0.8 to 1.2 (can vary)

The blue line is read using the left Y-axis.

Right Y-Axis (Price Axis)

The right Y-axis represents the NIFTY price (LTP).

  • Shows actual index levels (example: 25,980 – 26,060)

The grey dotted line is read using the right Y-axis.

Formula of Put–Call Ratio (PCR)

Formula:

Put–Call Ratio (PCR) = Total Put Open Interest ÷ Total Call Open Interest

Example:

  • Total Put Open Interest = 24,00,000
  • Total Call Open Interest = 20,00,000

Calculation:
PCR = 24,00,000 ÷ 20,00,000 = 1.20

What PCR Values Mean?

PCR Range Market View
> 1.2 Bullish
0.8 – 1.2 Neutral
< 0.8 Bearish

How traders use it?

  • When PCR is low (more calls than puts), traders feel the market is optimistic or aggressive. Some will go with the trend (stay bullish), while contrarian traders may become cautious because too many people are on the same side.
  • When PCR is high (more puts than calls), traders feel the market is fearful or hedged. Trend followers may stay bearish, while contrarians may start looking for a bounce if the value is extremely high.
  • Many traders don’t use PCR alone; they combine it with price trend, support–resistance and OI buildup to decide whether the sentiment shown by PCR matches the price action or is getting too extreme.

Limitations of Put–Call Ratio (PCR)

  • PCR changes as open interest changes; it is not a fixed value.
  • PCR can give misleading signals in strong trending markets.
  • News events and sudden volatility can reduce the reliability of PCR.

PCR shows sentiment, not certainty.
