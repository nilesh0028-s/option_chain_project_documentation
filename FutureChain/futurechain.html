What is FutureChain?

A FutureChain chain shows the list of available future contracts for a stock/index across different expiray date.

What is Future Contract?

A futures contract is an agreement to buy or sell an asset at a predetermined price on a specific future date. Unlike spot trading (buying and selling today), futures allow you to trade based on where you expect prices to go in the future.

Coloum Explanation

Expiry

This column shows the contract expiration dates. Each row represents a different futures contract. In your screenshot, you can see contracts expiring on 30-DEC-2025, 27-JAN-2026, and 24-FEB-2026. After the expiry date, the contract automatically closes and settles at the spot price.

OI (Open Interest)

Open Interest represents the total number of outstanding futures contracts that haven't been closed or settled yet. A high OI indicates that many traders are actively holding positions in that contract. In your data, the 30-DEC-2025 contract has the highest OI at 1,64,26,650, meaning it's the most actively traded (most liquid) contract.

Change in OI

This shows how much the Open Interest has increased or decreased from the previous trading session. For example, the 30-DEC-2025 contract increased by 77,775 contracts (0.48%), indicating fresh buying or selling activity in that contract.

LTP (Last Traded Price)

This is the most recent price at which the futures contract was traded. It represents the current market consensus of what the Nifty will be worth at that future date. For the 30-DEC-2025 contract, the LTP is 26,148.

Change in LTP (%)

This shows the percentage change in price from the previous trading day. The 30-DEC-2025 contract increased by 138.5 (0.53%), meaning the futures price went up by about 0.53% compared to yesterday's close.

High & Low

These columns show the highest and lowest prices at which the contract traded during the current session. For 30-DEC-2025, the High was 26,424 and the Low was 25,857, showing the intraday price movement range.

Rollover Cost

Rollover Cost is the extra amount you pay to move your position from the current month futures contract to the next month futures contract.

Formula:- Next Month Futures Price − Current Month Futures Price

Example

Imagine this situation

You rent a shop for December.

December rent = ₹10,000

Now December is ending, and you want to continue the shop in January.

January rent = ₹10,500

So what extra do you pay to continue?

₹10,500 − ₹10,000 = ₹500

That extra ₹500 is the ROLLOVER COST.

You didn’t buy a new shop.
You just extended the same shop for next month, but at a higher price.

Rollover Percentage

Rollover % shows how many traders decided not to exit their position at expiry, but instead continued the same trade in the next month contract.

Example

Suppose 100 traders are holding December futures.

When expiry comes:
• 70 traders shift their trade to January
• 30 traders close their positions

Rollover % = (70 / 100) × 100 = 70%

Meaning:
→ Most traders continued their trade
→ Indicates strong confidence in the next month’s trend

Key Differences Between Future Chain and Option Chain

What Each Chain Shows

Future Chain displays a list of all available futures contracts for the same underlying asset (like Nifty) across different expiration dates. Each row shows one futures contract with the same characteristics—only the expiry date changes.

Option Chain displays all available option contracts for the same underlying asset, organized by strike prices and contract types (Call & Put). Each row represents a different strike price, with call and put options shown side by side.

Core Conceptual Differences

Aspect Futures Options
Obligation You MUST buy or sell on expiry—no choice You have the RIGHT but NOT the obligation
What You're Choosing Only the expiration date Expiration date + strike price (entry level)
Upfront Cost No premium—only margin required Premium paid upfront
Maximum Loss Unlimited Limited to the premium paid
Profit Potential Unlimited Limited by premium structure

Structure and Layout Differences

Future Chain Structure

• Simple and linear — one row per expiration date
• All contracts track the same underlying price
• Shows OI, LTP, High/Low across different dates only
• Fewer variables to analyze

Option Chain Structure

• Complex table with Calls on one side and Puts on the other
• Multiple strike prices (₹26,000, ₹26,100, ₹26,200, etc.)
• Shows Greeks (Delta, Gamma, Theta, Vega)
• Hundreds of choices even for one expiry

How They Work

Futures Example

If you buy the 30-DEC-2025 Nifty futures at 26,148, you are obligated to:

• Take settlement at expiry, or
• Square off the position before expiry

If Nifty rises to 27,000, you profit. If it falls to 25,000, you lose the same amount. Your profit or loss is unlimited.

Options Example

If you buy a 26,200 Call option expiring on 30-DEC-2025 by paying a ₹50 premium, you get the right to buy Nifty at 26,200.

You can:
• Exercise it if profitable
• Let it expire if not

Your maximum loss is limited to the ₹50 premium.

Key Advantages

Future Chain Advantages

• High liquidity
• Lower transaction costs
• No premium — direct price exposure
• Simple to understand
• Best for strong directional trades

Option Chain Advantages

• Limited risk
• Flexibility in strike and expiry selection
• Can profit from time decay
• Useful for hedging and advanced strategies
• Better in uncertain markets

Decision Guide

Choose the Future Chain if you are confident about market direction and want direct exposure with high liquidity.

Choose the Option Chain if you want limited risk, flexibility in strategy, or protection against uncertainty.
